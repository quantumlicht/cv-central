Hello World!

Welcome to my home page. I am trained as a engineering physicist, but started my career as a software developer since 2012.

My passion about physics always came from the fact that I was fascinated by computers. I wanted to understand them so very badly, which eventually pushed me toward physics because in order to understand a computer, you also need to understand the smallest components, namely the transistors. I've always been fascinated by Moore's law, and the more I read on the subject, the more I understood we were on the verge of a radical paradigm shift with the advent of quantum mechanics and the difficulties of miniaturization at the nano scale.

This sparked the idea that I could participate in this revolution. Being the young optimist that I was and still am, I thought we were quite close from having quantum computers. Then came the harder check when I started my degree and I learned the hard, cold truth about quantum mechanics. It turned out that we were still a few decades away from what I had dreamed of and althought it was fascinating, I frankly did not want to spend my life in front of a black board, deriving the theory of this revolution. I wanted a more hands on approach, which ultimately led to my current carreer. "If I can't play with quantum computers yet, I might as well explore what's going on right now. It's not like there is no challenges left."
